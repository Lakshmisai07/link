Indian Foreign Service

About

The Indian Foreign Service (IFS) is the administrative diplomatic civil service under Group A and Group B of the Central Civil Services of the executive branch of the Government of India. It is a Central Civil service as Foreign policy is the subject matter and prerogative of Union Government.

Recruitment Process

Preliminary Examination:

IFS 2019 Preliminary exam is the first stage of the selection process.The Preliminary exam of IFS consists of two papers having 200 marks each. As per the selection process of IFS 2019, the Preliminary exam has Multiple choice questions (MCQs)The mode of examination will be offline (pen & paper based). Candidates qualifying in the Preliminary exam are eligible to appear in the Mains exam.

Mains Examination:

Mains exam is the second stage of the UPSC IFS 2019 Selection Procedure. Candidates qualifying in the cut-off list of the Prelims are eligible to appear for the Mains exam. The applicants will be tested on different parameters through the written test comprising of six papers and 1400 marks. Candidates who score the minimum qualifying marks in the Main exam are called for an Interview/Personality test.

Interview:

Personal Interview is the third stage of the IFS Selection Process. Candidates qualifying in the Mains exam are called to participate in the Personal Interview. Candidates in this round will be tested on their personality and their thought process combined with their knowledge about the subject matter and the post for which they have applied. A panel of experts will judge each candidate individually taking a maximum of 10 to 15 minutes. The total marks for the Personal Interview will be 300.
